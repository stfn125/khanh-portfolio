RoomRover
Project Overview
RoomRover is a mobile application designed to assist individuals with low incomes in finding suitable and affordable accommodation. The app aims to help reduce the stress and challenges associated with the housing search process by providing users with a user-friendly platform to explore various housing options within their budget. The app serves as a platform connecting landlords and renters, streamlining the process of property search, booking, and contract negotiation
MY ROLE
Research, Analysis Solution, Interaction Design, Design Systems, UI Design
DURATION
August 2023 - December 2023
TOOLS
Figma, Illustrator
Problem
Low-income individuals often struggle to find affordable housing options due to limited resources, lack of access to comprehensive listings and risk of being scammed. Traditional methods of finding accommodation, such as classified ads or word-of-mouth, are usually time-consuming and inefficient. Additionally, navigating through the complexities of rental agreements and understanding housing regulations can further exacerbate the difficulties faced by this demographic. Otherwise, unable to schedule in person property visits is a major obstacle for those who are with limited time availability
Solution
Design a user-friendly platform that simplifies the process of finding and securing affordable accommodation; providing detailed listings, including rent prices, amenities, and location details, allowing users to make informed decisions. Mainly focusing on simplifying the renting process, connecting renters with landlords, and helping users save their time
Key features include:
Step-by-step Contract: Users can fill out rental contracts through a guided process, eliminating confusion and promoting understanding

User Research
Summary
Through extensive user research, I conducted interviews, surveys, and user testing sessions to gather insights and feedback directly from the target audience. And with my personal experience as a low income individual struggling searching for good accommodation, I identified the following pain points among individuals:
→ Limited access to affordable housing options
→ Complex and intimidating rental agreements
→ Difficulty in scheduling property visits due to conflicting work schedules

Design Process
Sketch
I began the design process by sketching my ideas on paper. Sketching allowed me to visualize the layout, carefully consider the placement of elements and features, and readily make adjustments to the design as required
Wireframe
After sketching out initial concepts on paper, I progressed to developing low-fidelity wireframes using Figma. These wireframes became instrumental in honing the design, providing a tangible framework for the app's layout, structure
Low Fidelity Prototype
Time to craft a low fidelity prototype for the app. Using Figma, I translated my initial wireframe into a functional yet simplified representation of the app's interface and user flow. This prototype served as a crucial intermediary step, allowing for early user testing and feedback collection before committing to more refined designs
Style Guide
High Fidelity Prototype
In this phase, I elevated the visual design of the app. Incorporating detailed graphics, refined typography, and polished animations. I meticulously translated the finalized UI designs into interactive prototypes. This stage allowed for a comprehensive simulation of the app's functionality, enabling stakeholders to experience key features in a realistic context
